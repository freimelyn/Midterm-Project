SAUD BEACH RESORT PATAPAT VIADUCT
Saud Beach is known for its pristine condition, rustic appeal, and relative isolation from the tourist crowds. It is often dubbed as the “Boracay of the North”. A row of coconut trees along Saud Beach adds a tropical theme to the already beautiful resort destination that is Pagudpud. The Patapat Viaduct connects Maharlika Highway from Ilocos Region to its neighboring province Cagayan Valley. The bridge is elevated 31 meters (102 ft) over sea level. It can last up to forty-five years since it was built.
AGUA GRANDE PARAISO NI ANTON
Agua Grande known for its eco-friendly and non-carbon producing mini-hydropower plant, The park has two cemented swimming pools that utilize the flow of the stream that empties into the South China Sea. Paraiso ni Anton is a popular stop in Pagudpud known for its interesting history. It is part of the municipality's local tourism project. According to the urban legend, Anton appears to be a "healer" who used to have healing missions around the neighborhood.
MACAGUTOGOT USS STINGRAY SUBMARINE MEMORIAL
The Makagutugot Cave in Brgy. Pasaleng, Pagudpud has become the newest destination. They call it Palawan of the North because this cove boasts a stretch of rocky beach with ginormous, stunning rock formation boardered by crystal clear turquoise waters. In secluded Caunayan Bay, this memorial commemorates the mission of American submarine USS Stingray, which delivered weapons to Ilocano guerrillas, thus playing a decisive role in the Japanese defeat in WWII. An anchor on top but the surrounding scenery of palms, secluded white beach and rice fields makes it worth the trip.

HAKUNA MATATA PANZIAN BEACH RESORT
Nestled in one of the most peaceful beach front villages in Pagudpud near the northern tip of Luzon, Hakuna Matata offers picturesque views of awe-inspiring sunsets. It is a well known resort in Pagudpud. Pannzian Beach is steeped in history, and our family, the Ortegas, have been part of that history for hundreds of years, passing on a legacy of unspoiled natural beauty and strong community values. Pannzian Beach still evokes the same peaceful atmosphere since the 18th century.
APLAYA ORTEGA BEACH RESORT BLUE LAGOON AND BANTAY ABOT
Hidden gem in Pagudpud. Aplaya Ortega is a rare gem of a resort, located where the beach meets the rainforests of Pasaleng, Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte. Its swimming pool is fed by a natural waterfall cascading from the rainforests. From the inside of the hole one can see the blue waters of the Blue Lagoon. Apart from the view, one could also enjoy the birds nesting on the ceiling. The Blue Lagoon is a cove that is home to white sand and blue waters.
300 STEPS MARIAN HILLS VIEWDECK KABIGAN FALLS
he viewdeck is strategically located, allowing visitors to appreciate the beauty of the municipality from an elevated perspective. Kabigan Falls is a natural waterfall and tourist attraction in Brgy. Balaoi, Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte, Philippines. Featuring a cascade of approximately 87 feet (27 m) high that drops into a concave basin, Kabigan Falls is hidden in a dense forest and is accessible via a trek along a trail near Bantay Abot Cave.
